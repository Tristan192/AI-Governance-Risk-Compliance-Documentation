# AI Governance Maturity Assessment

This folder contains resources and templates for assessing the maturity of AI governance practices within an organization.

## Contents

### Templates and Models
- **5-Level Maturity Model (CMMI).xlsx**: A spreadsheet outlining a 5-level maturity model based on the Capability Maturity Model Integration (CMMI) framework, adapted for AI governance.
- **AI Governance Maturity Assessment Template.xlsx**: A comprehensive template for conducting maturity assessments, including scoring criteria and evaluation rubrics.
- **Assessment Criteria Per Domain.xlsx**: Detailed criteria for assessing maturity across different AI governance domains such as ethics, risk management, compliance, and operations.

### Reports
- **Executive Maturity Report.docx**: A sample executive report summarizing the findings of a maturity assessment, including recommendations for improvement.

## How to Use

1. **Start with the Maturity Model**: Review the 5-Level Maturity Model to understand the progression from basic to optimized AI governance practices.

2. **Customize the Assessment Template**: Use the AI Governance Maturity Assessment Template as a starting point. Adapt it to your organization's specific context and requirements.

3. **Define Assessment Criteria**: Refer to the Assessment Criteria Per Domain spreadsheet to ensure comprehensive coverage of all relevant governance areas.

4. **Conduct the Assessment**: Work through each domain, scoring current practices against the defined criteria.

5. **Generate Reports**: Use the Executive Maturity Report template to communicate findings to leadership and stakeholders.

## Key Domains Typically Assessed

- **Strategy and Leadership**: Alignment of AI initiatives with business objectives and executive commitment.
- **Ethics and Responsible AI**: Frameworks for ethical AI development and deployment.
- **Risk Management**: Identification, assessment, and mitigation of AI-related risks.
- **Compliance and Regulatory**: Adherence to relevant laws, regulations, and industry standards.
- **Data Governance**: Management of data used in AI systems.
- **Model Development and Deployment**: Processes for building, testing, and deploying AI models.
- **Monitoring and Auditing**: Ongoing oversight and audit capabilities for AI systems.
- **Skills and Culture**: Organizational capabilities and cultural readiness for AI governance.

## Best Practices

- Involve cross-functional stakeholders in the assessment process.
- Use the maturity model to identify quick wins and long-term improvement goals.
- Regularly reassess maturity as AI capabilities and regulations evolve.
- Tie assessment results to actionable improvement plans with clear timelines and responsibilities.

## Next Steps

After completing the assessment:
1. Prioritize areas for improvement based on gaps identified.
2. Develop a roadmap for advancing maturity levels.
3. Implement targeted initiatives to address critical gaps.
4. Schedule regular reassessments to track progress over time.
